Introduction

Urbanization is a defining phenomenon of the modern era, with cities expanding rapidly to accommodate growing populations and industrial demands. As urban areas continue to grow, they significantly reshape natural landscapes, impact the environment, and challenge sustainable development. Predicting how urbanization will evolve in the future is crucial for effective urban planning, resource management, and the mitigation of environmental impacts. This project focuses on using remote sensing data from the MODIS (Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer) satellite, combined with machine learning techniques, to analyze urban growth patterns and predict future urban expansion trends. The MODIS dataset offers a reliable source of geospatial data, with high temporal resolution and global coverage. This makes it an ideal choice for tracking urbanization trends over time and modeling future patterns. By leveraging machine learning algorithms in Python, this project aims to forecast urban growth based on historical data, enabling urban planners and policymakers to anticipate future challenges and strategize accordingly. Machine learning is particularly effective in this context due to its ability to handle large-scale datasets, identify complex patterns, and make accurate predictions. Urbanization prediction is a highly relevant topic because it has direct implications for infrastructure development, environmental conservation, and the allocation of resources. For example, unchecked urban expansion can lead to deforestation, habitat destruction, and increased greenhouse gas emissions. On the other hand, accurate predictions can help policymakers design sustainable urban layouts, minimize environmental impacts, and improve the quality of life for urban residents. This research will contribute to the growing field of urban studies by exploring how advanced computational techniques can provide actionable insights into one of the most pressing global challenges. Previous studies on urban prediction have often relied on traditional statistical models or low-resolution datasets, which limit their accuracy and scalability. Recent advancements in machine learning, including Random Forests, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and Deep Learning methods such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), offer powerful tools for urban prediction. By applying these methods to the MODIS dataset, this project seeks to improve the accuracy of predictions and provide a robust framework for analyzing urbanization dynamics. The ultimate goal is to enable decision-makers to plan for a sustainable urban future.
